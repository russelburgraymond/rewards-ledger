Getting Started

Use this page after a fresh install, after moving to a new machine, or any time you want to confirm the app is configured correctly before entering real data.

Do not blindly add defaults again. RewardLedger may already include common setup data such as BTC, GoMining Token, USD, GoMining accounts, and common categories.
Step 1: Confirm database connection

RewardLedger uses your config.php database settings. Make sure the host, username, password, and database name are correct for your local environment.

  • If the database does not exist yet, create it first in phpMyAdmin or your preferred MySQL tool.
  • After the connection works, RewardLedger can create or update its tables.
  • If onboarding is available, work through it instead of manually forcing tables unless you know exactly what you are doing.
  • When editing a ledger item, saved Time Received and Value at Time Received values are loaded back into the edit form.
Step 2: Review existing defaults

Before adding anything, open each of these pages and look at what already exists:

  • Assets
  • Accounts
  • Categories
  • Apps
  • Dashboard Settings

If defaults already exist, use them instead of creating duplicates.

Step 3: Add miners and custom accounts

Add only the records that are unique to you, such as:

  • Your miner IDs
  • Your custom wallets or cash accounts
  • Any extra apps beyond the defaults
  • Any categories that are truly specific to your workflow
Step 4: Choose your entry method

Quick Entry

Best for normal day-to-day manual entry.

AI Import

Best for screenshot conversion and paste-import workflows.

Templates

Best for repeated, structured multi-line entries.

Ledger

Best for review, correction, filtering, exports, and editing saved rows including received time and value-at-receipt details.

Step 5: Make a backup before major edits

Once the app is configured the way you want, create a backup. Do this before schema changes, major data imports, or broad cleanup work.
